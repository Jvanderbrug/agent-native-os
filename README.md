# Agent Native OS — Welcome to Your New Operating System

You're about to build something that will change how you work forever.

By the end of this weekend, you'll have a personal AI operating system — a setup that lets you direct Claude like a highly capable team member who knows your business, remembers your preferences, and can take real action on your behalf. Not just answering questions. Actually doing things.

This isn't a coding workshop. You don't need to be technical. If you can send an email and use Spotify, you have the skills you need.

---

## What This Repo Is

This is your workshop home base. Think of it as the binder they hand you at the start of a seminar — except instead of a binder full of slides you'll never open again, this one becomes a living part of how you work.

Everything you build this weekend lives here:
- Your personal `CLAUDE.md` — the file that tells Claude who you are
- Guides for each session, with step-by-step instructions
- Templates you'll customize
- Exercises matched to your track
- Troubleshooting help when things get weird

You'll keep this repo after the workshop. It grows with you.

**Day 2 — Sunday, 11 AM–5:15 PM EDT**

## The Three Tracks

You've been placed in one of three tracks based on your background. Don't worry about being in the "right" one — all three end up in the same place. They just take different paths to get there.

| Track | Who It's For | Pace |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **Beginner** | New to terminal, new to Claude Code | Slower, more hand-holding, more context |
| **Builder** | Comfortable with tools, some tech experience | Moderate, build while you learn |
| **Architect** | Technical background or prior AI tool experience | Fast, more customization, more depth |

Your track exercises are in the `tracks/` folder.

---

## What You'll Have by End of Day 2

- Claude Code installed and running on your machine
- A `CLAUDE.md` that gives Claude full context about who you are and how you work
- At least 2 MCP servers connected (Gmail, Calendar, Notion, Slack — your choice)
- Secrets stored securely (no API keys floating in plain text)
- An Obsidian vault set up as your knowledge base
- At least one custom slash command you built yourself
- A workflow running on a schedule without you touching it
- The mental model to keep building after you go home

## How to Use This Repo

**Before the workshop:** Do the pre-workshop guide at `guides/00-pre-workshop.md`. It takes about 30 minutes and will save you a lot of time on Day 1.

**During the workshop:** We'll move through `guides/01` through `guides/10` together. Follow along, and use the track exercises in `tracks/[your-track]/exercises.md` when we hit exercise time.

**When something breaks:** `resources/troubleshooting.md` covers the most common issues. Check there first before raising your hand — odds are someone already hit it and we fixed it.

## Prerequisites

Before Day 1, you need:

- A **Claude Max subscription** (~$100/month) — this is what powers everything
- A **GitHub account** (free at github.com) — where your code and files live
- A **Mac or Windows PC** — see `setup/mac/` or `setup/windows/` for your checklist

Full setup instructions: `setup/mac/prerequisites.md` or `setup/windows/prerequisites.md`
